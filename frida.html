Frida Kahlo

Artist · Symbol of Strength & Identity

1907 – 1954

Frida Kahlo was a Mexican painter known for her many portraits, self-portraits, and works inspired by the nature and artifacts of Mexico. Her work has been celebrated internationally as emblematic of Mexican national and indigenous traditions, and feminist movements.

Key Achievements

  • Created 143 paintings, 55 of which are self-portraits
  • First Mexican artist featured in the Louvre
  • Her work inspired the feminist art movement globally
  • Posthumously became one of the best-selling female artists in history
  • Subject of the acclaimed biographical film 'Frida' (2002)

"I never painted dreams. I painted my own reality."

— Frida Kahlo

Legacy

Frida Kahlo's unapologetic self-expression continues to inspire artists, activists, and women worldwide. Her image has become a symbol of resilience, feminism, and the beauty of being fully, painfully alive.
