Benazir Bhutto

First Female PM · South Asia's Trailblazer

1953 – 2007

Benazir Bhutto was a Pakistani politician who served as the 11th and 13th Prime Minister of Pakistan — the first woman to lead a Muslim-majority country. She was twice elected as Prime Minister and became a symbol of democracy in Pakistan.

Key Achievements

  • First woman to lead a Muslim-majority country (1988)
  • Twice elected Prime Minister of Pakistan (1988–90, 1993–96)
  • Graduated from Harvard and Oxford
  • Championed democracy and women's rights in Pakistan
  • Assassinated in 2007 while campaigning for democratic elections

"You can imprison a man, but not an idea. You can exile a man, but not an idea. You can kill a man, but not an idea."

— Benazir Bhutto

Legacy

Benazir Bhutto broke every barrier placed before her — gender, culture, religion, politics. She showed the world that women could lead nations. She remains an enduring symbol of courage, sacrifice, and the fight for democracy in South Asia.
