Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Supreme Court Justice · The Notorious RBG

1933 – 2020

Ruth Bader Ginsburg was an American lawyer and jurist who served as an associate justice of the Supreme Court of the United States from 1993 until her death in 2020. She was a pioneering advocate for gender equality and women's rights.

Key Achievements

  • Second woman appointed to the U.S. Supreme Court (1993)
  • Argued landmark gender equality cases before the Supreme Court
  • Co-founded the first law journal focused on women and the law
  • Became a pop culture icon known as 'Notorious RBG'
  • Authored numerous landmark opinions on equality and civil rights

"Women belong in all places where decisions are being made."

— Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Legacy

Ruth Bader Ginsburg spent her career dismantling legal barriers for women and minorities. She became a cultural icon — her 'dissent collar' a symbol of speaking up — and her life's work continues to shape American law and society.
