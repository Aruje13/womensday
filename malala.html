Malala Yousafzai

Activist · Educator · Nobel Peace Prize Winner

Born 1997

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. After surviving a Taliban assassination attempt in 2012, she became a global symbol of the fight for girls' right to education.

Key Achievements

  • Survived a Taliban assassination attempt in 2012
  • Youngest Nobel Peace Prize laureate (2014)
  • Founded the Malala Fund, advocating for girls' education
  • Addressed the United Nations at age 16
  • Author of 'I Am Malala'

"One child, one teacher, one book, one pen can change the world."

— Malala Yousafzai

Legacy

Malala's story inspired millions worldwide to fight for the right to education. The Malala Fund has helped millions of girls access schooling. She proved that a single voice, even when threatened with silence, can echo around the world.
