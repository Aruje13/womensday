Katherine Johnson

NASA Mathematician · Hidden Figure

1918 – 2020

Katherine Johnson was an American mathematician whose calculations of orbital mechanics as a NASA employee were critical to the success of the first and subsequent U.S. crewed spaceflights. She was a Hidden Figure who broke both racial and gender barriers.

Key Achievements

  • Calculated trajectory for Alan Shepard's 1961 space flight
  • Verified John Glenn's orbital mission calculations by hand
  • Co-authored 26 research reports at NASA
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (2015)
  • NASA's Katherine G. Johnson Computational Research Facility named after her

"I don't have a feeling of inferiority. Never had. I'm as good as anybody, but no better."

— Katherine Johnson

Legacy

Katherine Johnson's story, brought to global attention by the book and film 'Hidden Figures', showed the world the invisible contributions of Black women to America's space program. Her legacy lives on in every woman who dares to do the math.
