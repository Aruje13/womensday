Ada Lovelace

Mathematician · World's First Programmer

1815 – 1852

Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician who worked on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. She is often regarded as the first to recognise that the machine had applications beyond pure calculation and as the first computer programmer.

Key Achievements

  • Wrote the first algorithm intended for a machine (1843)
  • Recognised the Analytical Engine's potential beyond calculation
  • The programming language 'Ada' was named after her (1980)
  • Collaborated with Charles Babbage on the Analytical Engine
  • Her notes were rediscovered and hailed as visionary 100 years later

"That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal; as time will show."

— Ada Lovelace

Legacy

Ada Lovelace is celebrated every year on Ada Lovelace Day — a global event honoring women in STEM. She envisioned the future of computing over 100 years before it became reality, making her one of the most visionary thinkers in history.
