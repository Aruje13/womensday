Rosa Parks

Activist · Icon of Resistance · Mother of the Civil Rights Movement

1913 – 2005

Rosa Parks was an activist in the civil rights movement best known for her pivotal role in the Montgomery Bus Boycott. Her refusal to give up her seat to a white passenger on a segregated bus on December 1, 1955 became an international symbol of resistance to racial segregation.

Key Achievements

  • Refused to give up her bus seat in Montgomery, Alabama (December 1, 1955), sparking the Bus Boycott
  • The Montgomery Bus Boycott lasted 381 days and ended bus segregation
  • Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom (1996)
  • Received the Congressional Gold Medal (1999)
  • Became the first woman to lie in state at the U.S. Capitol (2005)

"People always say that I didn't give up my seat because I was tired, but that isn't true. No, the only tired I was, was tired of giving in."

— Rosa Parks

Legacy

Rosa Parks' quiet act of defiance ignited one of the most significant movements in American history. She showed the world that one person's courage can change the course of history. Her legacy is a reminder that standing — or sitting — for what is right can move mountains.
